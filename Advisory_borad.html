Dr. Anusha Edirisinghe

Member - Advisory Board

Dr. Anusha is a highly accomplished and respected educationist with over 25 years of experience in the Education industry in Sri Lanka. Currently serving as the Director of the Centre for Gender Studies at the University of Kelaniya, she has made significant contributions to the field of sociology and criminology. Having earned her BA in special sociology, criminology, and criminal justice system, Dr. Anusha went on to complete her MA in Criminology and subsequently pursued a PhD in the same field at Sri Jayawardenepura University. Her educational background has provided her with a solid foundation in understanding the complexities of social structures, criminal behavior, and gender equalities.

Dr. Anusha's expertise extends beyond academia, as she serves as a Senior Lecturer in the Department of Sociology at the University of Kelaniya. Her commitment to education is evident in her role as a lecturer, where she instills knowledge and critical thinking skills in her students, preparing them for success in their careers. In addition to her academic roles, Dr. Anusha also holds the position of Vice President at the Asia Crime Prevention Foundation Sri Lanka. In this capacity, she plays a crucial role in promoting crime prevention initiatives and fostering safer communities. Her dedication to creating a more secure society has been highly recognized and appreciated by her peers.

Beyond her professional achievements, Dr. Anusha is a role model and inspiration to many aspiring academics and educators in Sri Lanka. Her commitment to excellence, tireless work ethic, and compassionate approach to teaching have earned her the respect and admiration of her colleagues and students alike. In conclusion, Dr. Anusha is a seasoned educationist, highly regarded for her expertise in sociology, criminology, and gender studies. With her extensive experience and impressive achievements, she continues to make a significant impact in academia and contribute to the development of a safer and more equitable society.
